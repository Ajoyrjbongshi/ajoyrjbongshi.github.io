About Me

I am a graduate student in the Master of Science in Mathematical Sciences (Statistics) program at Clemson University. My academic interests lie primarily in survival analysis, biostatistics, causal inference, machine learning, and clinical research. I enjoy statistical computing, data visualization, and method development for high-dimensional data.

I have authored research on energy poverty prediction, mental health epidemiology, and statistical modeling for public health data. As both an instructor and teaching assistant, I enjoy mentoring students and helping them build strong quantitative foundations.
